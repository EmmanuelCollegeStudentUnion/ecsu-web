IMPORTANT: Society contacts are not all current members of the committee! The bank account signatories have not yet been updated (forms have been lost in the internal post at NatWest, causing severe delays to the mandate change application). Please do not contact the previous executive unless necessary.

Figures are forecasts based upon previous years, hence are rounded and not exact. Our budgeting year runs from Easter Term to Lent Term (out of sync with the academic year).

The society operates on a claims based treasury system - e.g. ensemble reps purchase music out of their own pockets and claim for reimbursement of the same amount. We are currently mid-way through the accounting year and have already had Â£1511.77 income from multiple sources, but claims for purchases are still being made - hence the budget cannot be adjusted, as there is no accurate authoritative data on all purchases made by the society (this is usually only complete by the end of the budgeting year). Claims are also still outstanding from the previous year - one individual has made claims which could not be accepted instantly (e.g. due to mathematics errors or insufficient evidence), and I am expecting to process these later this term.

The intention is to use ECSU funding to maintain our instrument and equipment collection. One such expense that is expected is cabling for the PA system - this is incredibly old and most of it is unusable. Spare drum parts (e.g. drum heads, hardware such as wing nuts) are also needed, as these are fragile or easily lost.
